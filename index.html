Discover the magic of Dulce de Leche

a sweet and creamy delight enjoyed all around the world. Also known as caramelized milk, milk candy, or milk jam, is a Latin American confection made by slowly heating sugar and milk for hours. The spreadable, sauce-like result boasts a rich flavor and color

History

Dulce de leche, a delicious caramel-like confection, has a rich history that spans several centuries. with disputed origins claimed by Argentina, Uruguay, and Mexico. Some historians also traces an Indonesian variation's path from the Philippines to Spain to the Americas, where it gained widespread popularity.

Let's learn more about how it's made, and the many ways you can use in different recipes.

To make dulce de leche, combine sugar and milk, then heat them slowly for several hours. Stir regularly to prevent sticking and burning.
The mixture gradually thickens and darkens, resulting in a caramel-like spread. Dulce de leche can be enjoyed in various forms, such as a topping for desserts, a filling for pastries, or a sweet dip for fruits.

The recipe can be customized with different types of milk or added flavors, creating a unique and delightful treat.
